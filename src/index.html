Ronald Wilson Reagan


Before Donald Trump, Ronald Reagan was the oldest person to assume U.S. presidency. He is also the only actor to become president. His remarkable skill as a charming orator earned him the title "The Great Communicator." People credit his policies for contributing to the demise of Soviet communism. His anti-communism sentiment was certainly a factor to the worsening of relations with the Soviet Union during the beginning of his presidency. U.S.-Soviet relations did improve, however, during Reagan's second term in office. In terms of domestic policies, Reagan proposed massive tax cuts (up to 30%) in both individual and corporate income taxes, massive increase in military expenditures, and significant funding cuts in social-welfare programs such as food stamps, low-income housing, education, Medicaid, etc. It's interesting to note that Reagan himself was raised in a poor family.

Key Events

  • January 20, 1981: Reagan is inaugurated.
  • January 20, 1981: 52 Americans held hostage in Iran are released (444 days)
  • February 18, 1981: Reagan proposes increased defense spending and decreased taxes and dosmetic spending in a speech to Congress.
  • March 30, 1981: Reagon is shot by John Warnock Hinckley Jr.
  • April 11, 1981: Reagan leaves the hospital. However, it would take months until Reagan fully recovered.
  • April 24, 1981: Reagan nominates Sandra Day O' Connor, the first woman on the Supreme Court.
  • August 13, 1981: Reagan signs a tax cut into law
  • September 3, 1982: Reagan signs the Tax Equity & Fiscal Responsibility Act.
  • January 5, 1983: Reagan signs a law that increased the gasoline tax by 5 cents per gallon.
  • April 20, 1983: End of the recession with the US GNP showing dramatic growth in the first quarter of 1983.
  • April 20, 1983: Reagan signs the Social Security Reform Bill
  • October 25, 1983: US forces invade Grenada in order to overthrow the military government that had taken over several days prior. US easily won.
  • August 22, 1984: Reagan is renomiated for the 1984 presidential election by the Republican Party.
  • November 6, 1984: Reagan is reelected as President. He defeated Democratic candidate Walter Mondale.
  • January 20, 1985: Reagan is inaugurated for a second term.
  • July 13, 1985: Bush is acting President for 8 hours while a malignant polyp is removed from Reagan's colon.
  • November 19, 1985: Reagan and Mikhail Gorbachev hold a summit meeting in Switzerland.
  • December 12, 1985: Reagan signs the Gramm-Rudman deficit reduction bill.
  • October 22, 1986: Reagan signs a tax code revision into law.
  • November 13, 1986: Reagan tells Congress that the US has been secretly selling arms to Iran, which violated federal laws against arms deal with Iran.
  • November 25, 1986: Reagan admits that the $10 million to $30 million in arms sales were diverted from Iran to the Nicaraguan contras (Iran-Contra affair)
  • February 3, 1987: Reagan vetoed the Water Quality Control Act but is overriden by Congress. EPA is allowed to regulate pollutants in US waters.
  • March 4, 1987: Reagan openly takes reponsibility for US actions in the Iran-Contra affair in a televised speech. He claims it happened without his knowledge.
  • December 7, 1987: Reagan meets with Gorbachev in Washington D.C. to sign the Intermediate Range Nuclear Forces Treaty.
  • January 29, 1988: Reagan prohibits federal abortion assistance. It was illegal for federally-funded family-planning centers to provide assistance to women seeking abortions.
  • November 8, 1988: VP George HW Bush is elected President. He won against Michael Dukakis.
  • January 11, 1989: Reagan says bye.

After his second term, Reagan and his second wife Nancy moved back to Los Angeles, California. In 1994, Reagan himself revealed that he was recently diagnosed with the Alzheimer's disease. He died on June 5, 2004 at the age of 93.
